The overachiever's delight: my first Perfect Score

22 May 2023

Whilst in Sydney on a work trip last week, I had this gut feeling while fetching a coffee that I should check my Coder Academy emails... just in case. And when I logged in, I didn't believe my eyes. A perfect score... my first ever perfect score.

To set the scene, a mere few weeks ago I was feeling incredibly stressed. I didn't feel like I got Python, at least to the level that I needed for this final assignment of the term. And heck, even my friend who is a Head of Data at a tech company was telling me that my app idea was ambitious, and that if I tried his app idea instead I could have punched it out in a day.

Eventually my app was working, I had built all my features! The final hurdle was testing... and boy oh boy, was it a wake-up call. Either my tests were failing, or I couldn't even begin to write a test for a function because of everything else going on inside it.

I take Friday off work to put my head down and get these tests working. I spent hours glued to my computer and feel like I'm getting nowhere. I pluck up the courage to ask for help on Discord, and the response only discourages me further. Just over 48 hours until the submission is due and I feel defeated. I go to the gym and out for dinner with some friends, and reassure myself that tomorrow is a new day.

On Saturday, something clicks. I manage to process the advice I received on Discord and with a clear head have the realisation - my code may be DRY and my app may be functional, but these functions are simply TOO BIG! And so the great refactor begins... I tear apart every function into anywhere from 2-5 new functions. I create another class so I can retain and set a few key variables between the different functions. And then over the course of about 8 hours, I manage to go from 2 basic test cases to 7 tests, most with multiple cases. It's working, I'm getting it!

The next morning, I wake with purpose - it's the final stretch. Time to smash out my documentation and call it a day. I even get to enjoy a few hours out with my friends in the afternoon, which felt delightful after being glued to my computer for the previous 2 days. I proudly submit, knowing I've comfortably covered the requirements - but accepting that there is always room for improvement.

The term break came at a perfect time, as I had a work trip to Sydney which I knew would be a challenge to balance with study. I check my student emails on Monday afternoon before my trip - nothing new. I don't keep these emails logged in on my phone, and I'm not really expecting to get my marks back until I return to Melbourne anyway. But on that brisk Wednesday morning before a busy day with my team, my gut tells me to login. And there I see the email - "Assignment Marked". I hold my breath as I click the link to login to our student platform. All I want is an HD, just an HD please...

100.5/100.5 - there it is. I'm shocked and think my eyes are fooling me so I click into the details and scroll, expecting to see constructive feedback. Instead, I see every HD section in the rubric highlighted, without a single point deducted. I'm speechless! Naturally, I message my Head of Data friend just to tell him that my ambitious idea worked.

Does this mean that I love Python and really want to focus my career on that? Honestly, it feels too soon to say. But this assignment taught me that in a world where you're planning an idea and all its features while also being the one to do all the coding and testing - go with what you know and/or love. My idea was ambitious but it was much easier to visualise because I was inspired by my sport and an existing website. In reality unless you're a one-person-band - you'll have Product Managers planning your features, Project Managers organising your deliverables, Team Leads to help plan increments and assign tickets... I won't have to do it all, and that's the way I like it.
